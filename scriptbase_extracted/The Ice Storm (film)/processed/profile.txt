The Ice Storm
The mood of The Ice Storm is tense, gloomy, and captivating.
The plot centers around suburban life, innocence lost, and a crumbling marriage.
It is a drama and independent movie.
Stylistically, The Ice Storm is episodic, is a melodrama, and includes a voice over.
In approach, it is serious and realistic.
The pacing is slow.
The Ice Storm takes place, at least partly, in the suburbs.
The setting is Connecticut.
It happens in the 20th century.
The Ice Storm is based on a book.
The movie is known for being a Cannes festival winner and critically acclaimed.
Note that it includes drugs/alcohol, sexual content, and profanity.
