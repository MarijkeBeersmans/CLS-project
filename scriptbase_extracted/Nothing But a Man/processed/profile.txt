Nothing But a Man
The mood of Nothing But a Man is thought provoking, clever, and emotional.
The plot centers around discrimination, interracial relations, and Americana.
It is a drama, independent, and romance movie.
Stylistically, Nothing But a Man is a melodrama.
In approach, it is serious and realistic.
The pacing is slow.
Nothing But a Man takes place, at least partly, in a small town.
The setting is Alabama.
It happens in the 1960s.
Visually, Nothing But a Man is black and white.
The movie is known for being a Venice Festival winner and critically acclaimed.
