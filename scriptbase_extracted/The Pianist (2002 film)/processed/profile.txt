The Pianist
The Pianist can be described as bleak, contemplative, and gloomy.
The plot revolves around the Holocaust, a conspiracy against humanity, and antiheroes.
The main genres are drama, independent, and music-oriented.
In terms of style, The Pianist is epic.
In approach, it is serious and realistic.
The storytelling is slow paced.
The Pianist is set, at least in part, in a ghetto.
It is located in Europe.
It takes place during World War 2 and in the 1930s.
The soundtrack of The Pianist is classical.
It is originally a true story.
The movie has received attention for being a modern classic, a Cannes festival winner, and an Oscar winner.
Note that The Pianist involves profanity and violent content.
